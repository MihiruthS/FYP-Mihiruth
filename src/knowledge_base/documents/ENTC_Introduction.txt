The Department of Electronic & Telecommunication Engineering at the University of Moratuwa is dedicated to imparting both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in electronic and telecommunication engineering. The department strives to stay up-to-date with the rapid technological advancements in the field and contributes to knowledge through high-quality research. The mission is to produce graduates who are multi-faceted and capable of succeeding in both national and international arenas. With a focus on bridging academic theory and industry needs, the department ensures that its graduates are equipped with the knowledge and skills required in todayâ€™s fast-evolving technological landscape.

The department offers two undergraduate honors programs: Electronic and Telecommunication Engineering and Biomedical Engineering. In addition to these, it offers postgraduate programs leading to M.Sc., M.Phil., and Ph.D. degrees. Currently, the department enrolls approximately 400 undergraduate students, ensuring a vibrant learning environment. The courses are structured to provide a strong foundation in theoretical concepts during the early semesters, while the later stages emphasize system-level knowledge. Students are encouraged to develop a mindset that connects different course modules and understands how they contribute to professional and technological leadership roles in the future.

Graduates from the department have access to a wide array of career opportunities in fields such as telecommunications, embedded electronics, software development, robotics and automation. Close collaboration with the industry ensures that students are well-prepared for cutting-edge roles in areas like embedded systems, the Internet of Things (IoT), robotics, and artificial intelligence (AI). Emerging trends in data services, automation, smart devices, cloud computing, and AI are transforming industries, and the department ensures that its graduates are well-equipped to enter these dynamic fields.

The department boasts a variety of state-of-the-art facilities that support both undergraduate and postgraduate learning. These include well-equipped laboratories that offer hands-on experience with advanced technologies. The Analog Electronics Lab provides oscilloscopes, signal generators, and other testing equipment for practical learning. The Computer Lab contains over 20 computers that support student assignments and project work. In the Digital Electronics Lab, students focus on microprocessor hardware, software design, programmable logic, and automatic testing. The Postgraduate Lab is equipped with sophisticated tools like logic analyzers and spectrum analyzers to aid in advanced research. The Telecommunication Lab focuses on communication circuits, radio signal modulation, and detection, while the Microwave Lab provides specialized training in microwave communication systems. Additionally, the Intelligent Machines Lab and UAV Research Lab focus on robotics, automation, and unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), while the Wet Experiment Lab supports multi-disciplinary experiments in fluids, biology, automation, and robotics. The Electronic Workshop allows students to gain practical experience in circuit design, soldering, and testing, enhancing their hands-on skills.

The academic staff of the department is led by the Head of Department, Dr. Thayaparan Subramaniam. The team includes a mix of professors, senior lecturers, lecturers, and technical officers with expertise spanning digital electronics, embedded systems, AI, automation, telecommunications, and other key areas of electronic and telecommunication engineering. This experienced and diverse faculty ensures that students receive a high-quality education that is both academically rigorous and practically relevant. A full list of staff members and their contact details can be obtained from the department office for those who wish to engage directly with the academic community.

Students at the department are encouraged to lead a balanced life, excelling not only academically but also in extracurricular activities. There are numerous opportunities for collaboration, research, and leadership development, allowing students to thrive in both academic and non-academic settings. Participation in social responsibility projects, sports, and recreational activities is strongly encouraged, helping students grow holistically and develop the soft skills that are essential for professional success.
